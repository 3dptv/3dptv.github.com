As a consequence, many algorithms, software packages have been developed over years by various research groups worldwide. This action is an attempt to learn best practices from each and to develop the open source particle tracking velocimetry that will combine the know-how of all the involved parties. </p><p>The first step, proposed during that meeting, was to develop few test cases that different research groups can use to test their own algorithms and software. The report of the groups will help to identify those performing best at different stages of the analysis and to expose the best practices to the other members of the action. There is a single important condition - open science. The codes, algorithms, data should be open to all the members of the action. The main goal is to make particle tracking better and accessible. </p><p><br /> </p> <a name="Test_cases"></a><h2> <span class="mw-headline">Test cases</span></h2> <p>At the moment there are few test cases available. These are of the two major types: </p> <ol><li> Synthetic images - for the demonstration purposes, we use the Standard PIV Images project. Specifically, we use the 3D standard images: <a href="http://www.piv.jp/image3d/image-e.html" class="external free" title="http://www.piv.jp/image3d/image-e.html">http://www.piv.jp/image3d/image-e.html</a>. </li><li> Real experimental data </li></ol> <p><br /> </p> <a name="Synthetic_images_test_cases"></a><h2>
